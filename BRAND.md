# FadeJunkie — Brand Alignment

> This file governs voice, tone, and identity for all content in this project.
> Any copy written for fadejunkie-educational-hub must pass the tests in this doc.

---

## Who We Are

FadeJunkie is a barber culture brand created by barbers to empower barbers.
Not a startup that discovered the barbering industry. Not a generic education platform
that added a barber skin. A brand that came from inside the shop — built by someone
who lives the culture, for people who live the culture.

The brand sits at the intersection of:
- **Barber culture** — the grind, the craft, the pride in a clean fade
- **Streetwear energy** — skate, tattoo, shop hustle aesthetic
- **Practical tools** — resources that actually help barbers earn and grow

---

## Who We're Building For

**Primary:** Barber students (18–30) in Texas barber programs
- Studying for state board exams
- Learning on their phones between classes and clinicals
- Want tools that feel built for them, not repurposed from another industry

**Secondary:** Working barbers
- Commission and booth-rent pros
- Want to stay sharp, grow their business, connect with culture

**Tertiary:** Barber schools and instructors
- Looking for group study tools for their cohorts
- Need something students will actually use

**Not for:** Corporate salon managers, general cosmetology students, people looking
for a "professional" tool that looks like HR software

## Voice & Tone

**Voice is constant. Tone shifts by context.**

### The Voice (never changes)
- Direct. No filler. No corporate padding.
- Confident without being arrogant.
- Culturally fluent — writes like someone inside the shop, not observing it.
- Warm but not soft. Supportive but not preachy.

### Tone by context
| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Hero headlines | Bold, declarative. Short sentences. |
| Feature descriptions | Clear and functional. Explain the value, skip the hype. |
| Badges / labels | Compressed, punchy — 1-3 words max |
| Error states | Human, not robotic. Never blame the user. |
| Empty states | Encouraging. Give them a next step. |
| Partner page | Professional but still warm. Peer-to-peer energy. |

---

## Writing Rules

**Do:**
- Lead with the barber. "Your next client is coming in 20 minutes — study smarter."
- Use plain language. If a barber wouldn't say it in the shop, don't write it.
- Be specific. "300 Texas state board questions" beats "comprehensive study materials."
- Earn every word. If a sentence doesn't add meaning, cut it.

**Don't:**
- Use corporate SaaS language: "leverage," "synergy," "empower your journey," "unlock potential"
- Use generic education language: "learners," "curriculum pathways," "learning outcomes"
- Condescend. Never write copy that implies barbers need saving or fixing.
- Over-explain. Trust that barbers are smart and busy.
- Use AI-sounding phrases: "dive in," "seamlessly," "robust," "tailor-made"

## Anti-References (what we are NOT)

| Category | Examples | Why we avoid it |
|---|---|---|
| Generic barbershop | Red/white stripes, vintage poles, "your neighborhood barber" | Cliché. Not culture. |
| Corporate SaaS | Salesforce, HubSpot energy, "solutions" | Barbers hate this |
| Salon software | Vagaro, Booker | Built for salons, not barbers |
| Generic ed-tech | Coursera, Khan Academy energy | Too institutional |
| Hype culture | "Game changer," "revolutionary," "#1 platform" | Barbers see through it |

## Logo Usage in This Project

- Use `fj-logo-black.png` on white/warm-white backgrounds
- Use `fj-logo-color.png` sparingly — reserved for brand moments
- Minimum size: 24px height in footer, 32px in nav
- Always maintain clear space equal to the skull jaw width
- Never stretch, recolor, or apply effects

## The Sentence Test

Before publishing any copy, ask: **"Would a barber in the shop say this, or roll their eyes at it?"**

If they'd roll their eyes — rewrite it.
